The Lewis Model: Communication Style

Multi-active & Reactive Blend

India is positioned between Multi-active and Reactive cultures.

  • Multi-active traits: Indians value warm personal relationships, flexible schedules, and expressive conversation.
  • Reactive traits: Preference for politeness, indirect responses, and careful listening.
Practical Advice: Belgian employees should be patient. Avoid direct confrontation. Allow conversations to develop naturally to build trust.

The Culture Map (Erin Meyer)

Navigating the behavioral scales between Belgium and India.

Communicating

Low Context High Context
Belgium
India

The Gap: Belgians are "Low Context": precise, simple, and clear. Indians are "High Context": sophisticated, nuanced, and implicit.

Tip: Don't just listen to the words. Read between the lines. If an Indian colleague hesitates, it likely means "No."

Evaluating

Direct Negative Indirect Negative
Belgium
India

The Gap: Belgians give frank criticism to help. Indians wrap criticism in praise to save face.

Tip: Never criticize openly. Use the "Sandwich Method" (Praise -> Correction -> Praise).

Leading

Egalitarian Hierarchical
Belgium
India

The Gap: In Belgium, the boss is a facilitator. In India, the boss is a respected authority figure.

Tip: Respect the hierarchy. Do not bypass your manager to speak to their boss.

Scheduling

Linear Time Flexible Time
Belgium
India

The Gap: Belgians focus on deadlines and punctuality. Indians prioritize flexibility and relationships.

Tip: Be patient with delays. A late start is often not a sign of disrespect, but of a different cultural priority.
